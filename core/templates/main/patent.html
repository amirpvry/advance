FAQ

Questions you often ask

For your invention to qualify for a patent registration

  • it must fulfill at least one of the following: Machinery and Mechanics: your invention must include parts that move (such as the gears in a watch or a car engine).
  • Articles of manufacture: Non-working part(s) of an object that stay in position. They might be fixed to the body of an object but could also be independent. Examples include bolts, screws, clips, etc.
  • Processes and methodology: Productions or objects that follow a set of pre-programmed functions (eg, the code in software; the handbook kept secret by a company to encourage competitiveness among its personnel).
  • Man-made chemical compositions and compounds such as pharmaceuticals, alternative health products or cleaning detergents.

You must get your patent registered as soon as your last prototype is finished. Why? If you bear the only copy, your local patent office will immediately give you approval.

You can put ‘patent pending on it as soon as you electronically submit your patent application to the patent office. That’s the notice to potential infringers.

A patent search confirms whether your invention is new, and helps to ensure that you have a good chance for your invention to be approved as patentable.

The patent draft is the body of your patent application, the meat, as it were. Your patent will function some predefined way. The patent draft has to explain exactly what that technical way is.

Unlike registration fees, patent annuities (the money paid to your local patent office each year after you are granted your patent) are called patent renewal or maintenance fees.

Failure to pay annuities upon the time fixed in the instrument granting the Patent to you will result in your rights … being forfeited.

A patent recordal is a standardised official record of all amendments to your application: change of ownership; change in name and/or address of owner or licensing availability.

Patent infringement refers to the unauthorised use of a patented invention, product or object.
